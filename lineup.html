USMC B-25 PBJ -Beth Jenkins

C130

The Lockheed C-130 Hercules is a four-engine turboprop military transport aircraft designed and built originally by Lockheed, now Lockheed Martin. Capable of using unprepared runways for takeoffs and landings, the C-130 was originally designed as a troop, medical evacuation, and cargo transport aircraft. The versatile airframe has found uses in a variety of other roles, including airborne assault, search and rescue, weather reconnaissance, maritime patrol, and aerial firefighting. It is now the main tactical airlifter for many military forces worldwide. Over 40 models and variants of the Hercules serve with more than 60 nations.

View Video

B17 Texas Raiders

Texas Raiders is one of the most recognized and famous Flying Fortresses currently on the airshow circuit. It has been recognized by AIR CLASSICS magazine as the best restored B-17G bomber currently flying in the world. The aircraft has been restored to wartime configuration by an entirely volunteer group of dedicated supporters. The aircraft has one of the most unusual histories of any existing Flying Fortresses flying today and is one of the most active and visible.

www.gulfcoastwing.org

A-26 Invader

The Douglas A-26 Invader was a United States twin-engine light bomber and attack aircraft built by Douglas Aircraft during World War II. With the powerful engines and on-board weapons, the plane also saw duties in Korea and Vietnam. This plane has seen service during several of the Cold War's major conflicts. We are very glad that Rich Gensheimer is back with us again.

Skybolt -Capt. Jeremy "Gunner" Holt

Full scale aerobatic flying seemed to be the next logical step in Jeremy "Gunner" Holt's ultimate quest to become an Air Show pilot. He received his tail-wheel training in a Decathlon and started working on basic aerobatics with his squadron commander who owns a Pitts S2A. He purchased the Skybolt in the spring of 2012 and it has been love ever since.

Falcon Flight

Falcon Flight is a Texas-based formation flying team, founded in the early '90s. All Falcon Flight team members fly experimental category RV aircraft constructed from kits from Van's Aircraft in Oregon. Most of Falcon Flight pilots built their own airplanes and they will thrill us with their precision again ! .

Extra 300L - Jan Collmer

This arobatic airframe is a two place, low wing monoplane. Jan is a former Navy Pilot and Dallas native with over 30 years experience. He performs in several military air shows every year and has over 400 air shows to his credit. High G rating.

www.Collmer.com/jcas/

P-40 Warhawk-Mike Anderson

The P-40 Warhawk was an American single-engine, single-seat, all-metal fighter and ground attack aircraft that first flew in 1938. While out classed by the Japanese Zero, the Warhawk held its own in dog-fights due to the durable nature of the airframe and the skill of the pilots. It was used by the air forces of 28 nations, including those of most Allied powers during World War II, and remained in front line service until the end of the war. The Flying Tigers used this aircraft exclusively prior to the United States declaring war on Japan.

T-33 - Kurt Langseth

The Lockheed T-33 Shooting Star (or T-Bird) is an American jet trainer aircraft. It was used by the U.S. Navy initially as TO-2 then TV-2, and after 1962, T-33B. Despite its age, the T-33 remains in service worldwide.

P-51 - Jimmy Fordham

Charlotte's Chariot II is fully restored to its flight glory. The sound of the aircraft in a shallow dive gave the enemy a reason to pause - Whistling Death! ! Aviation buffs believe the North American P-51 Mustang is the epitome of flight with its sleek design and 1,600-horse-power Merlin engine. This destictive aircraft still graces the skies and is a living piece of history.

Hi-24 ATTACK HELICOPTER - pETE COZ/BOBBI WILLIAMS

 This helicopter was developed by the Soviet Union as a counter-measure to the gunships and attack helicopters put in service by the US Army during the Vietnam War. The advantages of armed helicopter ground support doctrine with a mobile weapons inventory had a positive influence on the development of the Mil Hi-24.

T-6G "Texan" - Chip Lamb

A North American Aviation T-6G "Texan", SNJ, or Harvard as it was called in the British Empire, whose last official flight on active duty was March 26,1994. This aircraft was used to train pilots for active duty during the past 50+ years. The two-member crew capabilty and stable airframe was a forgiving platform for getting student pilots ready for the fighter aircraft. The precision of the Trojan Phlers is a sight to behold and makes us proud of the training of our Warriors of The Air. She has earned a well deserved retirement from active military service.
www.flsmicrojet.com

Sledge

T - 37 Tweet - eric stround

Matt Cessna's T-37 serves as a trainer around the globe for two-person crews. This unassuming aircraft is used through the armed forces world wide for training of student pilots. While not necessarily designed for stunts or aerobatic flight, Eric is very eager to show case this aircraft for us in the Air Show.

Twin Beech 18 -Matt Younkin

Matt Younkin’s Beech 18 performance is likely the most unusual act on the airshow circuit today.  The Beech 18 was never designed for aerobatic flight; however that doesn't make it incapable of doing just that.


The CCVF has been established to raise monies to assist with the physical and emotional recovery and rehabilitation of wounded, injured and disabled military personnel and veterans with a focus on those individuals residing in and organizations serving the Northeast Texas region. The Foundation’s principal fundraising activity is the Thunder Over Cedar Creek Lake air show presented during the July 4th Holiday weekend each year. The CCVF is an all-volunteer, non-profit 501(c)(3) organization. Your donation is tax-deductible as allowed by law..
